I don't have fish sauce — I'm making the Massaman Curry and only just noticed. What can I use?

Fish sauce is mainly there for its deep savoury umami — the fermented funk is secondary. Good news: you have several solid options that will work well in a Massaman:

Soy sauce + fresh lime BEST
Use ¾ the amount called for
Closest in umami depth. Add ½ tsp lime juice to mimic the fermented funk. Works perfectly in curry — barely noticeable difference.
2
Worcestershire sauce
Use 1:1 ratio
Good depth and complexity — it actually contains anchovies, similar base to fish sauce. Slightly sweeter but works beautifully in a Massaman with all those aromatics.
3
White miso + water
Use ½ the amount, diluted to liquid consistency
Excellent umami alternative. Will slightly change the colour to a lighter tone. White miso preferred over red — it's milder and less dominant. Also makes it vegan.

For Massaman specifically — go with the soy + lime combo. It'll be 90% as good and honestly most guests won't know the difference. Start with slightly less than the recipe calls for and taste as you go, since soy can be saltier than fish sauce.

Or: Use it completely undiluted for an even richer, more indulgent curry 🤌 — Pailin (Hot Thai Kitchen) actually prefers this approach. The Massaman will be noticeably creamier and more luxurious. Just reduce any added oil slightly since the cream is already high in fat.

Pro tip: If you have a can of coconut cream with solid cream on top and liquid underneath — that thick solid part is basically double cream. Use the whole can stirred together and you'll be in great shape.
