COOK MODE Step 1 of 8

Fill a large pot with enough cold water to fully submerge your 1.5 kg whole chicken. Add 3–4 slices of ginger and 2 spring onion stalks. Place on the stove over high heat.

⏱ ~8–10 min to boil
⏱ While the water heats up, now is a good time to take the chicken out of any packaging and let it come to room temperature if it hasn't already. Cold chicken straight from the fridge can lead to uneven cooking.
Water is boiling, chicken is in

Perfect timing! Now the critical part — reduce heat to the absolute lowest setting on your stove. You want lazy bubbles, not a rolling boil. The gentle heat is what creates the silky, impossibly tender Hainanese texture that makes this dish iconic.

If you're cooking on gas, turn it almost all the way off. On induction, go to level 2–3 at most. You should see just the occasional lazy bubble rising to the surface — nothing vigorous.

COOK MODE Step 2 of 8

With the chicken now in the water, immediately reduce heat to minimum. Cover the pot. You want the barest simmer — tiny bubbles occasionally breaking the surface. No more. Set a timer for ⏱ 30 min and do not lift the lid.

If you have a probe thermometer, target 75–80°C water temp
💡 This is a great time to start washing and soaking your 2 cups jasmine rice. Rinse until the water runs mostly clear — about 3–4 rinses. Let it soak while the chicken cooks.
