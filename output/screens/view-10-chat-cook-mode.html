COOK MODE Step 1 of 8

Fill a large pot with enough cold water to fully submerge your 1.5 kg whole chicken. Add 3–4 slices of ginger and 2 spring onion stalks. Place on the stove over high heat.

⏱ ~8–10 min to boil
💡 Take the chicken out of packaging and let it come to room temperature first. Cold chicken straight from the fridge leads to uneven cooking.
COOK MODE Step 2 of 8

With the chicken now in the water, immediately reduce heat to minimum. Cover the pot. You want the barest simmer — tiny bubbles occasionally breaking the surface. No more. Set a timer for ⏱ 30 min and do not lift the lid.

If you have a probe thermometer, target 75–80°C water temp
💡 Now is a great time to start washing your 2 cups jasmine rice. Rinse until the water runs mostly clear — about 3–4 rinses. Let it soak while the chicken cooks.
COOK MODE Step 3 of 8

After 30 minutes, turn off the heat completely. Keep the lid on. Let the chicken poach in the residual heat for another ⏱ 20 min. Do not open the pot.

💡 The trapped steam continues cooking the chicken gently — this is what gives Hainanese chicken its signature silky texture.
COOK MODE Step 4 of 8

Remove the chicken and plunge it into an ice bath immediately. Leave for ⏱ 10 min. This stops the cooking and tightens the skin. Reserve all the poaching broth — you'll use it for the rice and soup.

💡 The ice bath is not optional — it creates that glossy, taut skin. Don't skip it even if you're in a hurry.
COOK MODE Step 5 of 8

In a wok or saucepan, heat 2 tbsp chicken fat or oil over medium heat. Add 3 cloves minced garlic and 1 tsp minced ginger. Stir until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add the drained soaked rice and stir-fry for 2 minutes.

💡 Using rendered chicken fat instead of oil adds incredible depth to the rice. Skim it from the surface of your poaching broth.
COOK MODE Step 6 of 8

Transfer the rice to a rice cooker or pot. Add 2 cups poaching broth (instead of water), 1 pandan leaf, and ½ tsp salt. Cook until done — about ⏱ 15 min in a rice cooker.

💡 Cooking the rice in chicken broth instead of water is the secret that transforms ordinary jasmine rice into something extraordinary.
COOK MODE Step 7 of 8

While the rice cooks, prepare the dipping sauces. Blend 4 red chillies, 3 garlic cloves, 1 tbsp ginger, 2 tbsp lime juice, and 1 tsp sugar for the chilli sauce. Mix 3 tbsp soy sauce with 1 tsp sesame oil for the soy dip.

💡 Make the sauces fresh — bottled versions won't do this dish justice. The brightness of fresh lime and ginger is essential.
COOK MODE Step 8 of 8

Remove the chicken from the ice bath and pat dry. Chop into serving pieces or slice thinly. Plate over the rice, garnish with sliced cucumber and spring onion. Ladle the clear broth into bowls. Serve all sauces on the side.

✓ Recipe complete — enjoy!
💡 Serve warm rice, room-temperature chicken, hot broth, and cold sauces together — the contrast of temperatures is part of the experience.
