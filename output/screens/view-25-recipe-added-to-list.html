Hainanese Chicken Rice is Singapore's most iconic national dish — a deceptively simple preparation of silky poached chicken served over fragrant jasmine rice cooked in rich chicken broth.

Brought to Singapore by Hainanese immigrants in the early 20th century, this dish has been named one of the world's 50 most delicious foods by CNN Travel. Its genius lies in making every drop count — the poaching liquid becomes the rice broth.

🇨🇳 Chinese 🇸🇬 Singaporean 🏠 Comfort food 🌾 Gluten-free
Why it works
🌡️
Temperature control is everything
Poaching at barely a bubble keeps proteins relaxed for impossibly tender meat. Boiling toughens the flesh irreversibly.
🧊
The ice bath creates the silky skin
Shocking the hot chicken in ice water instantly contracts the skin, creating that taut, jade-green, gelatinous layer.
🍚
Rice cooked in broth, not water
The rice absorbs all the rendered chicken fat and poaching aromas — turning a simple side into the true soul of the dish.
You'll make
🍗 Poached whole chicken — sliced, glazed with sesame oil and soy
🍚 Fragrant ginger-garlic rice cooked in the poaching broth
🥣 Three dipping sauces: chilli, ginger-spring onion, and dark soy
