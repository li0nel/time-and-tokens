The chicken should have been poaching for about 30 minutes at a gentle simmer. Time to check how we're doing — this is the moment of truth for getting that silky Hainanese texture right.

COOK MODE Step 4 of 8

Lift the lid and insert a probe thermometer into the thickest part of the thigh, without touching the bone. You're looking for 74°C / 165°F. If it reads lower, cover and continue for 10 more minutes, then check again.

⚠️ Always check the thickest part of the thigh — not the breast. Breast cooks faster and will read higher. Thigh temp is the true indicator.
75°C — we're good!

Excellent! Perfect temperature. Now the most important moment in the whole recipe — the ice bath. This single step is what separates silky Hainanese chicken from ordinary poached chicken. Don't skip it, don't shorten it.

COOK MODE Step 5 of 8

Carefully transfer the chicken from the hot water to your prepared ice bath. Fully submerge it. Hold it down if it floats. Leave for exactly ⏱ 10 minutes. This shock-stops the cooking and contracts the skin to create the signature silky, bouncy texture.

⚠️ Don't skip this step — it's literally the difference between silky, bouncy Hainanese skin and flabby, sad skin. Have your ice ready before you start.
