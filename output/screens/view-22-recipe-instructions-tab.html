Hainanese Chicken Rice

Screen stays on · Timers included · Hands-free
1
Fill a large pot with cold water — enough to fully submerge the chicken. Add 4 slices fresh ginger and 4 spring onion stalks. Bring to a rolling boil over high heat.
2
Lower the whole chicken breast-side down into the boiling water. Bring back to a full boil, then immediately reduce heat to the absolute lowest setting. The liquid should barely simmer — small, lazy bubbles only.
3
Cover the pot and poach for ⏱ 30 min without lifting the lid. Maintaining consistent gentle heat is the single most important factor in silky texture.
4
Turn the chicken breast-side up using tongs. Poach for an additional ⏱ 10 min.
💡 Insert thermometer at the thickest part of the thigh (not touching bone) — look for 74°C / 165°F to confirm doneness.
5
Transfer chicken to an ice bath IMMEDIATELY. Fully submerge and leave for ⏱ 10 min.
⚠️ Do not skip the ice bath — it creates the silky, taut, jade-green skin that defines the dish. Skip it and the skin will be flabby and pale.
6
While the chicken poaches: heat 2 tbsp sesame oil in a wok or heavy saucepan over medium heat. Add sliced ginger and sauté for ⏱ 2 min until fragrant. Add the unwashed jasmine rice and stir to coat every grain in the oil.
7
Ladle in 4 cups of hot poaching liquid. Season with 1 tbsp light soy sauce and a pinch of salt. Cover tightly and cook on the lowest heat for ⏱ 18 min. Do not lift the lid.
8
Make the sauces. Chilli sauce: blend red chillies, garlic, lime juice, fish sauce and sugar until smooth. Ginger sauce: combine grated ginger, minced spring onion and salt in a bowl, then pour smoking hot neutral oil over the top — it should sizzle dramatically.
9
Remove chicken from ice bath. Pat thoroughly dry with paper towels. Rub all over with a mixture of sesame oil and light soy sauce. Carve: remove legs and thighs first, then slice the breast. Arrange on a platter, skin-side up.
10
Serve the sliced chicken over a mound of fragrant rice, with cucumber slices, all three dipping sauces, and a small bowl of clear poaching broth seasoned with white pepper and spring onion.
🍽️ Traditionally served at room temperature — the chicken is not meant to be hot. This is correct.
