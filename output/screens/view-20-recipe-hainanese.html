Notes (4)

Hainanese Chicken Rice is Singapore's most iconic national dish — a deceptively simple preparation of silky poached chicken served over fragrant jasmine rice cooked in rich chicken broth. Every element of the plate is carefully considered: the jade-tinged skin, the fragrant rice, the three essential dipping sauces.

Brought to Singapore by Hainanese immigrants in the early 20th century, this dish has been named one of the world's 50 most delicious foods by CNN Travel. Its genius lies in making every drop count — the poaching liquid becomes the rice broth, the rendered fat flavours the rice, and nothing goes to waste.

Why it works
🌡️
Temperature control is everything
Poaching at the lowest possible simmer — barely a bubble — keeps the proteins relaxed and produces impossibly tender meat. Boiling toughens the flesh irreversibly.
🧊
The ice bath creates the silky skin
Shocking the hot chicken in ice water contracts the skin instantly, creating that distinctively taut, jade-green, gelatinous layer that defines authentic Hainanese chicken.
🍚
Rice cooked in broth, not water
The jasmine rice absorbs all the rendered chicken fat and poaching aromas, turning a simple side into the true soul of the dish. You cannot replicate this with plain-cooked rice.
You'll make
🍗 Poached whole chicken — sliced, glazed with sesame oil and soy
🍚 Fragrant ginger-garlic rice cooked in the poaching broth
🥣 Three dipping sauces: chilli, ginger-spring onion, and dark soy
