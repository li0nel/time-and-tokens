About This Recipe
🇨🇳 Chinese 🍗 Chicken 🍚 Rice Dish 🌶️ Mild
Hainanese Chicken Rice is Singapore's national dish — a deceptively simple preparation of poached chicken served over fragrant rice cooked in the poaching stock. The magic lies entirely in the technique: slow poaching at 80°C creates an impossibly silky texture, and the ice bath finish creates the glossy skin the dish is famous for.
Origin & History

Originally from Hainan province, China, the dish was brought to Southeast Asia by Hainanese immigrants in the late 19th century. Singapore elevated it to an art form, with dedicated hawker stalls competing over their techniques.

The three dipping sauces — chilli ginger sauce, dark soy, and fresh ginger-scallion oil — are as important as the chicken itself. Every hawker has their own proprietary recipe.
