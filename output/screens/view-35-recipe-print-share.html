Hainanese Chicken Rice
20 min prep
1h 30min cook
4 servings
Medium
Ingredients
  • 1.5 kg Whole chicken, room temperature
  • 400g / 2 cups Jasmine rice, rinsed
  • 4cm piece Fresh ginger, sliced
  • 4 stalks Spring onions
  • 2 tbsp Sesame oil (toasted)
  • 2 tbsp Light soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
  • 1 tsp Sea salt
  • 1 litre Chicken stock
  • 6 Red chillies (for sauce)
  • 1 Lime, juiced
  • 1 tbsp Fish sauce
Instructions
  1. 1
    Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a rolling boil. Lower the whole chicken in breast-side down. Return to a boil, then immediately reduce to a very gentle simmer at 80–85°C.
  2. 2
    Add ginger slices and spring onion whites. Poach for 45–50 minutes for a 1.5 kg bird, or until juices run clear when the thigh is pierced at its thickest point.
  3. 3
    Remove the chicken and immediately plunge into an ice bath for 10 minutes. This stops cooking and tightens the skin to create the glossy texture the dish is known for. Reserve all poaching liquid.
  4. 4
    Heat sesame oil in a wok. Fry the rinsed rice with ginger until fragrant, about 3 minutes. Transfer to a rice cooker and add 2 parts poaching stock to 1 part rice. Cook until done.
  5. 5
    Blend chillies, garlic, ginger, lime juice, fish sauce, and a splash of poaching liquid for the chilli sauce. Taste and adjust seasoning to balance heat, salt, and acid.
  6. 6
    Slice the chilled chicken. Serve over a mound of fragrant rice with dark soy, fresh cucumber, chilli sauce, and ginger-scallion oil on the side.
