French Onion Soup

Soupe à l'oignon gratinée
20m
Prep
1h 30m
Cook
4
Serves
⭐⭐
Level
Method
1
Prepare your onions. Peel and slice 1.5 kg large yellow onions into thin half-moon rounds, approximately 3–4mm thick. Even slicing is important so all pieces cook at the same rate. Set aside in a large bowl.
2
Begin with a mirepoix of roughly diced onions, carrots and celery. The caramelisation of this aromatic base provides the foundation of the soup's flavour. Cook in butter over medium heat until completely softened and beginning to colour, about 20 minutes.
MIREPOIX
A classic French aromatic base of onion (50%), carrot (25%), and celery (25%), finely diced and gently cooked in fat. Named after the 18th-century Duc de Mirepoix. Used in stocks, soups, braises, and sauces as a flavour foundation.

3
Add the sliced onions to the pot and caramelise slowly over medium-low heat. Stir every 5–8 minutes for ⏱ 45–60 min until they are deep golden-brown — not burnt, not pale. This step cannot be rushed. Patience here determines the whole character of the soup.
4
Once the onions are deeply caramelised, deglaze with 200ml dry white wine, scraping up all the fond from the base of the pot. Allow the wine to fully reduce before adding stock — this concentrates the sweetness and removes any raw alcohol edge.
A dry white Burgundy or Sauvignon Blanc works best
5
Add 1.5 litres good beef stock and a bouquet garni of thyme, bay leaf, and parsley stalks tied in cheesecloth. Simmer gently for 20 minutes. Remove the bouquet garni. Taste and season carefully — the soup should be deeply savoury with a clean wine note.
6
Cut a baguette into thick diagonal slices, brush with olive oil, and toast at 180°C until very dry and hard. These croûtons must be firm enough to float on the soup without becoming soggy. They're the structural base of the dish.
7
Ladle soup into oven-safe bowls, top with croûtons, then cover generously with grated Gruyère or Comté. Place under the grill to gratiné — au gratin is the whole point of this dish. The cheese should bubble, blister, and turn deeply golden-brown in 3–5 minutes.
Watch it closely — it goes from perfect to burnt in 30 seconds
