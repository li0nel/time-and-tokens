Boeuf Bourguignon is the definitive French braised beef — slowly cooked in an entire bottle of Burgundy wine with lardons, pearl onions, and mushrooms until the beef becomes impossibly tender and the sauce transforms into something dark, glossy, and profound.

Julia Child introduced this dish to an entire generation of American home cooks through her 1961 cookbook and subsequent television series. Her genius was in demystifying French technique without sacrificing rigor. This recipe remains the definitive reference nearly 65 years later.

🇫🇷 French 🍷 Burgundy 🍖 Braise ❄️ Make ahead
Why it works
🍷
A whole bottle of wine, no shortcuts
A full bottle of Burgundy (or good Pinot Noir) is what gives this dish its depth. Never use "cooking wine" — if you wouldn't drink it, don't cook with it.
🥩
Patience transforms tough cuts
Chuck or brisket contains the collagen that converts to gelatin over long slow cooking, giving the sauce its luxurious, coating body. Tender cuts would disintegrate.
🍄
Garnish cooked separately
Mushrooms and pearl onions are sautéed separately and added at the end. They hold their shape and provide textural contrast to the silky braised beef.
You'll make
🥩 Deeply braised beef in dark, glossy Burgundy wine sauce
🧅 Lardons (bacon) and pearl onions integrated into the braise
🍄 Sautéed mushrooms added as garnish at the end
