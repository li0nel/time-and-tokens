Ingredients
1 kg
Beef chuck
Cut into 5cm chunks
2 × 400ml
Full-fat coconut milk
Chaokoh or Aroy-D
4 tbsp
Massaman curry paste
Mae Ploy brand recommended
300g
Waxy potatoes
Peeled and quartered
2
Shallots
Peeled and halved
60g

2 tbsp
Fish sauce
1 tbsp
Palm sugar
Or light brown sugar
2 tbsp
Tamarind paste
3
Bay leaves
Dried or fresh
Instructions
Crack the paste. Heat 3 tbsp coconut cream in a wok or large pot over medium-high heat. Add the Massaman paste and fry until the oil visibly separates from the paste and it turns deep orange, ⏱ 4–6 min. The kitchen should smell incredible.
2
Brown the beef. Add the beef chunks and sear on all sides, coating them in the fragrant paste. Work in batches if needed to avoid steaming. Each piece should have colour on at least two sides.
Pat beef dry before searing — moisture is the enemy of browning
3
Add the remaining coconut milk, peanuts, shallots, and bay leaves. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook covered for ⏱ 1h 30min, stirring occasionally. Add potatoes in the last 25 minutes.
✦
mise. Notes — 3 tips
PRO
Massaman is measurably better the next day. The overnight rest lets the beef fully absorb the sauce and the spices bloom further.
SHORTCUT
Mae Ploy Massaman paste is genuinely excellent. Use 3 heaped tablespoons per 1 kg of meat. The gap from homemade is small — 85% as good.
VARIATION
Try adding a small piece of ripe banana in the last 10 minutes. It melts completely into the sauce and adds a natural floral sweetness. Strange but excellent.
