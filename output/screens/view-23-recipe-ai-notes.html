SHORTCUT
Skip making all 3 sauces if short on time. Store-bought Sriracha works fine for chilli. For a quick ginger-soy dip: 2 tbsp light soy + 1 tsp sesame oil + 1 tsp freshly grated ginger. That's 80% of the flavour with 20% of the effort — perfect for a weeknight.
CHEF'S METHOD
Blanch the chicken in boiling water for exactly 30 seconds, immediately plunge into an ice bath, repeat 3 times before the main poach. This firms the skin, removes surface impurities, and results in a cleaner broth with noticeably glossier skin. Julia Child applied this technique to all poached birds.
VARIATION
Kampong (kampung) style: seek out a yellow-skinned free-range chicken — often sold as "kampung chicken" in Asian grocers. Add 2 pandan leaves to the poaching water. The result is more complex, floral, and markedly closer to what you'd find at a traditional Singapore hawker stall. Worth the extra effort to find.
COMMUNITY TIP
The leftover poaching liquid is liquid gold — use it the next morning for congee. Season with white pepper and fresh ginger, add day-old rice, simmer to a thick porridge. Users consistently rate the congee as the best part of making this recipe. Don't pour it down the drain.
