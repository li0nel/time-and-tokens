  11. How do I cook it properly?

  12. Three things that make Thai green curry exceptional:

    👨‍🍳

    Chef's Notes

    • Bloom the paste first. Always fry the curry paste in coconut cream (not milk) before adding anything else — this unlocks the aromatics.

    • Don't boil the coconut milk. Keep it at a gentle simmer or it will split and become greasy.

    • Balance at the end. Taste and adjust with fish sauce (salty), lime juice (sour), and palm sugar (sweet) before serving.

  13. What is mirepoix?

  14. In French cuisine, is a foundational aromatic base used in many dishes:

    Culinary term

    Mirepoix

    A mixture of diced onions, carrots, and celery (typically 2:1:1 ratio) used as a flavour base in soups, stocks, and braises. In Thai cooking, a similar aromatic base uses shallots, galangal, and lemongrass.

  17. Secrets to great Thai curry?

  18. The secrets come down to technique and ingredients. Explore by topic:

    • 🌱

      Lemongrass must be bruised

      Smash the lower third with the back of a knife to release the oils before adding to the wok.

    • 🧄

      Galangal ≠ ginger

      Galangal is piney and sharp — do not substitute with ginger unless you must. The flavour changes significantly.

    • 🍃

      Kaffir lime leaves: tear, don't cut

      Tearing releases more citrus oils than cutting. Add at the end for the brightest flavour.

  26. Interesting history?

  27. Thai curry has a fascinating origin:

    📜

    Did you know?

    Thai green curry (gaeng keow wan) was first recorded in Thai royal cookbooks from the early 20th century. The green colour comes from fresh green chillies — historically it was considered the hottest curry in Thai cuisine, though modern recipes are often milder.

    Source: The Oxford Companion to Food, Alan Davidson (2014)
