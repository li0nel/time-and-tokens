Massaman Curry

⏱ 90 min
🔥 650 cal

Ingredients

  • Beef chuck, cut into chunks 800g
  • Coconut milk 400ml
  • Massaman curry paste 4 tbsp
  • Potatoes, peeled and quartered 500g
  • Onions, cut into wedges 2 large
  • Fish sauce 3 tbsp
  • Palm sugar 2 tbsp
  • Tamarind paste 1 tbsp
  • Roasted peanuts 80g
  • Cinnamon stick 1
  • Cardamom pods, lightly crushed 4
  • Bay leaves 3

Instructions

  1. 1

    In a dry wok over medium heat, toast the cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, and bay leaves for 1–2 minutes until fragrant. Remove and set aside.

  2. 2

    Increase heat to high. Sear beef chunks in batches until browned on all sides, about 3–4 minutes per batch. Set aside. Do not crowd the pan.

  3. 3

    Reduce heat to medium. Add massaman curry paste and fry in the residual fat for 2 minutes, stirring constantly, until deeply fragrant and darkened slightly.

  4. 4

    Pour in coconut milk and stir to combine. Return seared beef and toasted spices to the pot. Add onion wedges.

  5. 5

    Bring to a gentle simmer, cover, and cook over low heat for 45 minutes, stirring occasionally, until beef is nearly tender.

  6. 6

    Add potato chunks and continue simmering uncovered for 20 minutes until potatoes are soft and the sauce has thickened.

  7. 7

    Stir in fish sauce, palm sugar, and tamarind paste. Taste and adjust — it should be richly savory, gently sweet, and mildly sour. Fold in roasted peanuts.

  8. 8

    Serve over jasmine rice. Garnish with extra roasted peanuts, fried shallots, and a drizzle of coconut cream if desired.

Variations

AI Notes

PC

Don't skip toasting the whole spices first — 90 seconds in a dry pan transforms cinnamon and cardamom from flat to aromatic. That base note is what makes Massaman distinct from other Thai curries.

From Pailin C. · recipe author

MR

For the beef, short rib works brilliantly here in place of chuck. The extra marbling means you get silkier sauce without any added fat — and it holds together better over the long simmer.

From Marco R.

PM

Making it vegetarian? Press the tofu for at least 30 minutes before cubing and browning. Use coconut aminos instead of tamari if you want a milder, sweeter profile without the soy.

From Priya M.

CL

The balance of fish sauce, palm sugar, and tamarind is crucial — taste near the end and adjust one at a time. A good Massaman should be all three: salty, sweet, and gently sour, with none dominating.

From Chef Lee

SW

This dish reheats even better on day two — overnight the spices mellow and the potatoes absorb the sauce. Make a double batch and refrigerate; skim the solidified coconut fat from the top before reheating.

From Suki W.
